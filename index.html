Traumacore and Recommendations

05/18/21


Just a couple months ago youtube started recommending me videos with names like weirdcore playlist and traumacore/glitchcore playlist. The content of each video was just a few songs played over a looping animation or some sort of animation that evolved over time like a changing color filter. Each playlist had similar songs and usually differed in just 4 or 5 of their song choices. Some recurring artists would include Mac Demarco, Sewerslvt, Sitcom, Kero Kero Bonito, Blank Banshee and a few others. The visuals accompanying any given video were usually simple and contained anime or hello kitty iconography. Stagnant visuals were a faux pas so to speak, it was paramount to keep the viewer entertained but not overstimulated. Even if it was just the color filter changing or the intensity of an after effects slider, it showed at least the relics of a clear thought and understanding of the digital medium. What I'm trying to say is that most of these videos had a lot in common, in a good way. I think it showed an understanding of the medium in which they presented themselves.

The name of the videos themselves told me that people resonated emotionally with this kind of broken and unstable motif. A glitch in itself refers to the broken nature of a thing or more specifically it's unstable inner workings, so its easy to make the mental connection between a glitch and something like mental trauma that can leave one feeling unstable or internally like there is something wrong. Everyday it's becoming more apparant that a generation of kids who were raised by the internet and had absent parents are coming of age and they want to create content that they can put out into the world. This desire to connect with people through a small playlist of songs and share common niche interests is part of the community building process as well as naming the community itself. I don't think hyperpop or soundcloud rap could have become as popular as they are without having been named. The name itself doesn't have to be catchy but it needs to exist in order for people to properly categorize their idea of the genre and in order to communicate about the feeling the genre gives them. By naming a thing we give ourselves the ability to compare and contrast it with other ideas.

I think the creation of these playlists is healthy for the overall music scene. It has at least led to me discovering one or two new artists. And this kind of organic exploration/discovery of new music is sort of akin to the tik tok music scene where we've already seen a few people manifest careers for themselves just by being discovered organically through the app, I'm thinking specifically of Pink Pantheress, whom I really quite like. It's really never been a better time to be a musician or curator I think in terms of your ability to reach people. As things only become more interconnected people may crave more generalized curation done by a human being as opposed to individualized curation done by an AI. Because the curation done by the AI will most likely keep you inside a safe bubble where it knows exactly what content you'll enjoy whereas a living breathing person may offer you something different, more jarring, more experimental maybe. And that kind of organic discovery is really the most rewarding for people. Then again I probably would not have even discovered these types of videos if it had not been for the youtube algorithm. Maybe then, like most things in life, it is about finding a balance and creating a feedback system between these two seemingly opposite ends of the spectrum: Algorithm recommendations and organic suggestions, one made by machines, the other by real people.

The names we can give to a genre truly are infinite especially when you consider the multitude of expressions we can concoct just by taking a word and adding -core or -pop to the end or maybe adding tech- and glitch- to the beginning but the names have never really mattered to me. Just the act of discovery is important to me and I'm willing to seek out new ways of discovering things. I wonder whether places like Youtube will continue to be sources for the discovery of new music or if it will become easier with new platforms that may overtake Apple Music and Spotify. Right now it remains to be seen but I can definitely say I hope the recommendations continue lol.
